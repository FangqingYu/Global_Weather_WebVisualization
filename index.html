Summary: Latitude vs. X

The purpose of this project was to analyze how weather changes as you get closer to the equator. To accomplish this analysis, we first pulled data from the OpenWeatherMap API to assemble a dataset on over 500 cities.


After assembling the dataset, we used Matplotlib to plot various aspects of the weather vs. latitude. Factors we looked at included: temperature, cloudiness, wind speed, and humanity. This site provides the source data and visualization created as part of the analysis, as well as explainations and descriptions of any trends and correlations witnessed.

Max Temperature

As expected, the weather becomes slightly warmer as on approaches the equator (0 Deg. Latitude). More interestingly, however, is the fact that the southern hemisphere tends to be warmer this time of year than the northern hemisphere. This may be due to the tilt of the earth at the time of the year this data was gathered.

Visualizations

Humidity

According to the visual, the humidity level tends to be more uniformly distributed and higher aroud the equator (0 Deg. Latitude) and varies more as one moves away from it. The northern hemisphere tends to be slightly more humid this time of the year than the southern hemisphere, but the difference is not big.

Visualizations

Cloudiness

According to the visual, the amount of overcast tends to be slightly varied around the euqater (0 Deg. Latitude), but the difference is not obvious. The amount of clouds in the sky tends to be random around this time of the year.

Visualizations

Wind Speed

According to the visual, the wind speed is slower around the equator (0 Deg. Latitude) and gradually picks up as one moves further away from it. It also looks like the wind speed is faster at the northern hemisphere than the southern hemiphere around this time of the year.
